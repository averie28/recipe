Chocolate Chip Cookies

These cookies are inspired by the cookies the Lara Jean made in the book "To All the Boys I've Loved Before" by Jenny Han when she finally perfected her recipe. Lara Jean's secret to this is using parchment paper and gourmet sugar but it's hard to find such a specific sugar, so for this recipe I just use regular white and brown sugar. I've made them a couple times before and they're one of the best cookies I've ever had. This recipe is pretty simple and the dough only takes about 15 minutes to make if you are using a stand mixer.

1 cup (2 sticks) salted butter, softened

Ingredients

1 cup white granulated sugar
1 cup light brown sugar, packed
2 teaspoons vanilla extract
2 large eggs
3 cups all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 teaspoon baking powder
1 teaspoon sea salt (optional)
2 cups chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Mix together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, blend the two sugars and butter together with an electric mixer. Add in the vanilla and eggs into the mixture and blend.
  3. Combine the contents of both bowls. Add in the chocolate chips.
  4. Spoon the dough onto a sheet of parchment paper, and shape the dough into a log. Fold the parchment paper over the dough and cover the dough and paper with plastic wrap. Refrigerate the roll for an hour.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius).
  6. Cut the log of dough into thin slices and roll each slice into a one-inch ball. Set them on a parchment-lined baking sheet two to three inches apart.
  7. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes, and allow the cookies to cool on a wire rack before eating.
